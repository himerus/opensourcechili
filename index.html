What makes a great chili?

Chili, or Chili con carne is a food unlike most others. Chili has a almost cult like following of connoisseurs that insist the chili they produce is by far the best anywhere.

Batch

Each batch makes approximately 5 quarts. For a single batch (default) I usually use my crock pot to let the batch simmer all day. However, when making larger batches for canning, I generally use a very large stock pot.

The Medium version (default) will produce a good runny nose, and make you sweat a fair deal. The mild still has a decent kick to it, while the hot version will make you intestines bleed.

Instructions

Chili is one of the easier one-pot meals to prepare, and the response you'll get from your lucky taste testers will have you on top of the world for minimal effort!

If you are making a single batch to be eaten the same/next day, I recommend using a large crock pot that will allow the chili to simmer over low heat for multiple hours before serving or refrigeration. However, if you are making multiple batches for a large group or event, or for canning, you'll need a large enough stock pot to accommodate the massive amount of ingredients.

  • Soak beans overnight in cold water.
  • Drain Beans, cover beans with hot water, and bring to a boil
  • Lower heat to medium, and let the beans slow boil until the are soft and the skin peels back when blowing on a bean.
  • Mix your cans of Crushed Tomatoes, Tomato Sauce, and drained Diced (or Petite Cut) Tomatoes, and water in stock pot and place over medium heat.
  • Combine all dry spices either in directly in the pot, or in a prep bowl. I prefer to measure out my spices the night before into a bowl that can quickly be added to the sauce as it speeds up the prep time on the day you are making the chili.
  • Chop Onion to a size of your preference and add to sauce. The Onion can be either added directly to the sauce, or cooked first with the Ground Beef & Italian Sausage
  • Brown all Ground Beef & Italian Sausage, drain and add to sauce.
  • Let simmer for 2-8 hours. If you are serving this chili the same day, let it simmer as long as possible. If you are canning the chili, the simmer process is not really needed, so an hour or two of simmer time would be more than enough before processing your jars.
  • Cut celery into 1" slices, and add to chili. For best results, only add the celery 30 minutes to 1 hour before serving if doing a single batch in a crock pot, or 30 minutes before placing chili in jars for canning. This ensures they stay as crisp and firm as possible.

Canning

When canning chili, I use my All American Pressure Cooker/Canner. There is no better pressure cooker/canner on the market. The lack of a rubber gasket/seal means the canner will be working just as well in 30+ years with only greasing the metal to metal seal every few uses.

For more information on canning, I highly recommend the following books to get you going. Preserving delicious meals for years to come is much easier than you might think!
