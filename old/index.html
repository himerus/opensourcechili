Everyone loves chili

Chili, or Chili con carne is a food unlike most others. Chili has a almost cult like following of connoisseurs that insist the chili they produce is by far the best anywhere.

Why try this Chili?

Well, you likely found this site/recipe in search of a chili recipe, so it seems like you've found what you're looking for! Give it a shot and you won't be disappointed!

Get Started

The following recipe will guide you through selecting the batch size and heat level, show you the appropriate ingredients, and walk you through the process of making the chili and optionally canning the chili for long term storage.

Each batch makes approximately 5 quarts. For a single batch (default) I usually use my crock pot to let the batch simmer all day. However, when making larger batches for canning, I generally use a very large stock pot.

Instructions

Chili is one of the easier one-pot meals to prepare, and the response you'll get from your lucky taste testers will have you on top of the world for minimal effort!

If you are making a single batch to be eaten the same/next day, I recommend using a large crock pot that will allow the chili to simmer over low heat for multiple hours before serving or refrigeration. However, if you are making multiple batches for a large group or event, or for canning, you'll need a large enough stock pot to accommodate the massive amount of ingredients.

Prepare Beans

  • Sort and rinse beans well.
  • Soak beans overnight in cold water.

Sorting and soaking should be done the night before you plan to make your chili. Cooked dry beans are so much better (and better for you) (and cheaper) than canned beans.

Cook Beans

  • Drain beans, cover with hot water, and bring to a boil.
  • Lower heat to medium, and low boil until they are soft (approximately 2 hours).
  • Drain beans, add to sauce.

While the beans are boiling, you can move on to starting the sauce/spice steps.

Make the sauce

Mix your cans of Crushed Tomatoes, Tomato Sauce, drained Diced Tomatoes, and water in stock pot and place over medium heat.

The Diced Tomatoes can be substituted with most types of tomato you might prefer more. I prefer smaller chunks of tomato in my chili, so I really prefer the Petite Cut variety, otherwise diced, stewed, or anything similar will work just fine. Use your imagination!

Measure the spice

Measure out and combine all dry spices in a prep bowl.

I prefer to measure out my spices the night before into a bowl that can quickly be added to the sauce as it speeds up the prep time on the day you are making the chili. Otherwise, it is fine to add spice directly to sauce as you measure it, just don't lose your count!!

Combine spice & sauce

Add all spices to the sauce.

Include any "wet" spices to the sauce as well, including the Worcestershire, Tabasco and Sriracha.

Chop onion(s)

Chop onion and add to sauce.

You can chop the onion to any size large or small that you prefer. You can also cook them with the meat in the next step before adding them to the sauce.

Chop Jalapeños

Chop (or slice) jalapeños and add to sauce. Jalapeños are only added if you are making a HOT batch. If you selected Mild or Medium above, you can skip this step.

I didn't have a real photo of Jalapeños from my last batch as my wife can't stand anything more than the medium heat (which she still complains is too hot), so this jalapeño photo is from my last batch of pickeled jalapeños.

Brown meat

Brown ground beef & sausage, drain, add to sauce.

If you are doing a smaller batch, this can be done in a single skillet, but may require either a ginormous skillet or doing several batches of meat.

Let it simmer

Let chili simmer for 2-8 hours.

If you are serving this chili the same day, let it simmer as long as possible. If you are canning the chili, the simmer process is not really needed, so an hour or two of simmer time would be more than enough before processing your jars.

Add celery

Cut celery into 1" slices, and add to chili..

For best results, only add the celery 30 minutes to 1 hour before serving if doing a single batch in a crock pot, or 30 minutes before placing chili in jars for canning. This ensures they stay as crisp and firm as possible.

Most people that have my chili think the celery is odd until they try it. It adds some "crisp" (literally) to the chili. You won't regret adding it!

  • Serve & Enjoy!

Canning

When canning chili, I use my All American Pressure Cooker/Canner. There is no better pressure cooker/canner on the market. The lack of a rubber gasket/seal means the canner will be working just as well in 30+ years with only greasing the metal to metal seal every few uses.

For more information on canning, I highly recommend the following books to get you going. Preserving delicious meals for years to come is much easier than you might think!
